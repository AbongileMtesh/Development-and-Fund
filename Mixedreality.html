Mixed Reality Simulation

What If Teachers Could Practise Before They Teach? Help fund SA’s first Mixed Reality Simulation for teacher training.

The University of Johannesburg’s Faculty of Education is seeking support to pilot a South African version of Mixed Reality Simulation (MRS), an innovative and proven approach to strengthening teacher education and professional development. By combining virtual environments with real-time interaction, MRS allows trainee teachers to practice and refine their skills in a safe, controlled, and highly engaging setting before entering real classrooms. This pilot is the critical first step toward building a permanent, homegrown MRS platform that can be expanded to universities across the country, ultimately creating a self-sustaining national resource that improves the quality of teaching at scale. The Faculty has already begun exploring the technology through TeachLivE and is actively researching its effectiveness, but funding is now needed to move from early experimentation to structured implementation. With donor support, this initiative has the potential not only to advance teacher training but also to make a lasting impact on the future of education in South Africa.
