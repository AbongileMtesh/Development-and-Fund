Missing Middle

Unable to Self fund. Too “Rich” for financial aid. Help close the funding gap for thousands of students who fall through the cracks.

60% +

of students are missing middle.

The “Missing Middle” refers to students whose families earn too much to qualify for government bursaries, yet too little to afford the full cost of university fees. These students are often caught in a difficult position, unable to access the financial support available to their peers while still struggling to cover essential expenses such as tuition, accommodation, and learning materials. At the University of Johannesburg, we recognise that the Missing Middle represents a significant portion of our student body and that their financial challenges can place immense strain on their academic journeys. By supporting initiatives that target the Missing Middle, we ensure that talented, hardworking students are not excluded from higher education simply because they fall outside existing funding models.
